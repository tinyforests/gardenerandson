Designing ecological gardens.
Building the systems around them.

Gardener & Son designs ecological gardens — tuned to soil, water, canopy, and habitat — and builds the measurement and registry layers that allow private land to function as connected ecological infrastructure.

Start a conversation View garden work

A studio operating in two directions.

The work begins in real gardens: reading a site, shaping land, building soil, and planting for long time. Beauty is treated as a by-product of alignment — not decoration.

From there, the work extends outward: classification, measurement, registry, incentives — the infrastructure that allows ecological gardening to be recognised, valued, and repeated.

Private land is one of the largest land classes in cities. When it is treated as a disconnected surface, ecological value stays invisible.

When it becomes legible — recorded and connected — a new kind of ecological economy becomes possible: stewardship with proof, and restoration that can scale.

Studio

Gardener & Son was founded by Tyson and Natasha in Melbourne. The studio brings together ecological design, systems thinking, and the economic architecture required to scale restoration through private land.

The work spans gardens, measurement frameworks, registry infrastructure, and the cultural narrative required to shift how cities understand private ecology.

Building the private ecological infrastructure layer for cities.

This requires more than gardens. It requires registry infrastructure and economic frameworks that can recognise, value, and connect private ecological work — without losing the craft and beauty of the garden itself.

This is the ecological economy: stewardship with proof, and restoration that can compound across neighbourhoods. Long-horizon work. Built steadily.
