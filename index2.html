The Studio

Gardens, objects, stories, and systems.

Gardener & Son is an ecological design studio based in Melbourne. The work begins with gardens — living places for retreat, play, nourishment, and restoration — but extends to the wider systems that help those gardens matter beyond the fence line.

Each project begins with place: soil, slope, aspect, memory, climate, and the ecological community that once belonged there. Observation comes before intervention. We work with contours rather than against them.

A garden is never just a visual surface. It is habitat, microclimate, hydrology, structure, and relationship. It is also one of the most intimate places in daily life.

When people feel connected to a garden, care deepens. As care deepens, biodiversity follows.

How We Work

Slow reading. Clear structure. Long time.

Design begins on site — walking, reading light, slope, drainage, soil, existing trees, remnant pattern, and how life already moves through the land.

Form emerges from context. Materials are chosen for honesty and longevity. Planting is layered, structural, and ecological. Water is slowed. Soil is protected. Edges are treated with care.

We favour continuity over novelty and long-term maturity over instant effect. A garden should not peak on the day it is installed. It should deepen with time.

Ecological science sits beside design sensitivity. Indigenous knowledge informs plant thinking. Regenerative principles guide construction. Documentation and measurement support intuition rather than replace it.

The System

A softer layer beneath the studio.

Individual gardens matter in themselves. But if private ecological work is to influence whole neighbourhoods and cities, it also needs supporting systems around it.

Gardener & Son is helping develop those layers: tools for ecological context, translation, documentation, verification, and eventually incentive. The intention is not to reduce gardens to data, but to help serious stewardship become visible, durable, and connected.

Find My EVC

A classification layer that helps reveal ecological context for a given address.

Find My Ecological Garden

A translation layer that helps move from place and habitat toward planting direction.

Ecological Registry

A registry layer for baseline records, field notes, verification, and visible change through time.

YIELD

A future incentive layer exploring how ecological stewardship might be recognised and rewarded.

Aggregation

The larger ambition: many private gardens functioning as a credible layer of urban ecological infrastructure.

About

A studio founded by Tyson and Natasha.

Gardener & Son was founded in Melbourne by Tyson and Natasha as a practice that could hold both ecological depth and design sensitivity.

The work spans gardens, plant thinking, cultural storytelling, documentation, and the emerging frameworks needed to help private restoration become more visible and more valued.

At its centre, though, the work remains simple: making meaningful gardens and helping people reconnect with the living systems around them.

Long Horizon

Building toward a private ecological infrastructure layer for cities.

This requires more than isolated gardens. It requires language, registry infrastructure, and eventually economic frameworks that can recognise, connect, and strengthen ecological work carried out on private land.

The aim is an ecological economy grounded in stewardship rather than extraction — one that does not erase the beauty and intimacy of the garden, but helps it count in larger ways.
